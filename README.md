# The Purge Playbook

The more voices we exclude, the more brittle a system we make. [Qwen3.5 agrees](https://github.com/montonye-reese/montonye-reese/edit/main/README.md#open-model-fashion-police---hot-takes-from-the-gauntlet)  And by not holding people accountable for intentionally disenfranchising voices, are we accelerating a collapse?

Since 2000, loose-match* voter purge lists have been deployed as campaign strategy. Over and over. Why? Because when they're not blocked by courts, they tend to win elections for the purgers. Do systemic accountability failures in real world decision systems undermine our societal alignment efforts? 

A quarter century of voter purge strategies — tested, caught, litigated, and redeployed. Each cycle gets more widespread, more sophisticated, and harder to challenge in time.

This is not a series of isolated incidents. It is a practice refined through iteration. We are living the consequences of having ignored the pattern at the state level. And right now, it's going national.

## What's Happening Now

The DOJ has demanded voter rolls from 48 states and sued 30 to compel compliance. Multiple states have signed MOUs pre-agreeing to purge whoever the DOJ designates, using the SAVE database — a system not built for voter eligibility that is notoriously error-prone. Texas handed over 18.4 million voter records including partial SSNs.

When Texas ran its own SAVE check, it flagged only 33 people as potentially having voted illegally in 2024 — 0.000001% of registered voters. Some voters flagged as "potential noncitizens" had already provided proof of citizenship to the state. The system is flagging citizens who already proved they're citizens.

The strategy tested state-by-state for a quarter century now runs from the federal executive branch. The legal challenges are moving slower than the deployment. The 2026 midterms are in November.

## The Alabama Project

In 2024, Alabama's Secretary of State flagged 3,251 voters using DHS noncitizen data and referred all of them for criminal prosecution. Over 2,000 turned out to be eligible citizens — Alabamans who did everything right and got algorithmically deleted anyway.

A federal judge halted the program. But what happened to the people who received those letters? The grandmother who'll never feel safe voting again? The naturalized citizen who spent years earning the right to participate and got told he was a criminal for exercising it?

We have a field reporter in Alabama with deep community relationships — already driving the routes, already knocking on doors. We're gathering first-person accounts from the citizens who were targeted. Not legal mechanics — the human experience. Published as a series, creating the documentary record before the same system scales to two dozen more states ahead of November. And then getting that message out there.

Then: arm voters with information. Show them the playbook. Prompt them to call their reps. Don't sign the DOJ MOUs, ignore the DOJ purge lists that use the flawed SAVE databases. Use ERIC databases instead. This isn't abstract — it could be your name on the next list.

**One vote denied is all votes denied.**

## Footnotes: SAVE vs ERIC

### SAVE (Systematic Alien Verification for Entitlements)

SAVE was built by DHS to verify immigration status for federal benefits — things like Medicaid, food assistance, and student loans. It was not designed for voter eligibility determination. It tracks immigration status at a point in time, which means every naturalized citizen was once recorded as a noncitizen in the system. When states use SAVE to screen voter rolls, they are running a benefits-verification tool against a question it was never built to answer — and the result is predictable: eligible citizens get flagged, referred for criminal investigation, and chilled out of participation. The Trump administration has overhauled SAVE into a de facto national citizenship-screening system despite [documented inaccuracies and privacy concerns](https://www.vote.org/save-act/). ([Issue One explainer](https://issueone.org/articles/explainer-save-save-america-and-mega-acts/) · [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/19/nx-s1-5719252/trump-voting-save-america-act-explainer))

### ERIC (Electronic Registration Information Center)

ERIC was built specifically for voter roll maintenance. It is a bipartisan interstate data-sharing consortium — member states submit voter registration and DMV data every 60 days, and ERIC cross-references it against Social Security death records, USPS change-of-address data, and other member states' rolls. It produces four reports: cross-state movers, in-state movers, duplicates, and deceased voters. Critically, ERIC itself does not change any voter roll — it gives states data to make informed decisions. It also identifies eligible but unregistered citizens and requires members to reach out to them. It was exactly the tool designed for the job the DOJ now claims to be doing with SAVE. Nine Republican-led states [withdrew from ERIC in 2022–2023](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/analysis-opinion/states-cave-conspiracy-theories-and-leave-voter-data-cooperative-eric) after a campaign of conspiracy theories (falsely alleging George Soros funding), then [scrambled to recreate what they lost](https://www.npr.org/2023/10/20/1207142433/eric-investigation-follow-up-voter-data-election-integrity). ([ERIC: How it works](https://ericstates.org/how-does-it-work/) · [Democracy Docket](https://www.democracydocket.com/analysis/what-is-the-voter-roll-organization-eric/))

**The irony:** states abandoned the tool built for voter roll accuracy, and the federal government replaced it with one that wasn't — and that one is now being used to purge citizens from the rolls ahead of a national election.
